# Amorphous Research

The browser I am sharing here is part of ongoing artistic investigation, which explores an amorphous research on the notion of dust. This project uses p5.js and custom HTML to simulate a forensic research desktop. The art project is a research for an ever-fluxing question, excavating for an ever-shifting answer, and witnessing the layers in between.

## Current situation of the Research Project

How is dust noticed when intervened by selected object? How to shape research by passing through the subject matter? The question will keep changing as the research semi-mutates according to different subject matter assembled in-between noticing dust.

Currently, the research is situated in a semi-mutating dialogue with different topics, that will affect methods orbiting around Dust and Rock. The documentation of Amorphous research is a key aspect, that is why I created an online platform to implement development of the research. Using the browser as a tool of research, and ability to change its logic manually have added a layer of experimentation in the process of this research.

## Desktop Features

Dynamic Windows\_ Draggable, resizable windows.

Microscopic Atmosphere\_ Interactive background particles of Dust that repel from the mouse and pulse.

Bottom Left\_
Open all windows at once a click in the left bottom (could crash the desktop, why not!) or Open 5 random windows
